San Mateo County: Nueva School ex-employee convicted for lewd texts to student

  • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
  • Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)
  • Click to email this to a friend (Opens in new window)
  • Click to print (Opens in new window)
By Nico Savidge | nsavidge@bayareanewsgroup.com | Bay Area News Group
PUBLISHED: February 7, 2019 at 11:18 am | UPDATED: February 7, 2019 at 12:15 pm

A former employee of aSan Mateo County private school accused of sending lewd texts and having inappropriate contact with a student will face up to six months in jail as part of a plea bargain.

Ian Thomas Whiteman, a 39-year-old Redwood City man, pleaded no contest Wednesday to a felony charge of contacting a minor to commit a felony, according to prosecutors.

Whiteman worked for two years in the information technology department of the Nueva School, a K-12 school with campuses in San Mateo and Hillsborough. He was fired after school officials learned in 2017 about his contact with a 14-year-old student.

In July and August of 2017, Whiteman texted the girl “at all hours of the day and night,” sending her “lewd and inappropriate messages,” according to the San Mateo County District Attorney’s Office. Prosecutors also alleged he “engaged in lewd conduct” toward the girl.

Authorities said the girl’s parents discovered the text messages and told school officials, who informed police.

Prosecutors charged Whiteman last year with five felony counts and one misdemeanor. The misdemeanor and all but one of the felony charges were dismissed under the plea agreement, which also caps the length of Whiteman’s potential jail sentence at 6 months.

He will be sentenced May 3.
